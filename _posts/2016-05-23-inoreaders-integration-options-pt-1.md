After Google’s announcement regarding Reader’s impending doom, I spent weeks trying out alternatives, including self-hosted offerings. I eventually settled on <a href="https://www.inoreader.com/">Inoreader</a>, after seeing someone recommend it in a comment on a post about Google Reader alternatives.

This post is regarding the “integration” options you can see at the top of each post in Column View (and at the bottom of each post in Expanded View) allowing you to send articles to different external services: <a href="http://getpocket.com">Pocket</a>, <a href="http://www.instapaper.com/">Instapaper</a>, <a href="http://www.readability.com/">Readability</a>, <a href="http://www.evernote.com/">Evernote</a>, <a href="http://office.microsoft.com/en-us/onenote/">OneNote</a>, and <a href="https://www.dropbox.com/">Dropbox</a>.

<a href="http://blogs.gundersons.us/josh/wp-content/uploads/sites/2/2016/05/article-top-buttons-in-Inoreader.jpg"><img class="aligncenter" style="background-image: none; float: none; padding-top: 0px; padding-left: 0px; margin-left: auto; display: block; padding-right: 0px; margin-right: auto; border: 0px;" title="article top buttons in Inoreader" src="http://blogs.gundersons.us/josh/wp-content/uploads/sites/2/2016/05/article-top-buttons-in-Inoreader_thumb.jpg" alt="article top buttons in Inoreader" width="244" height="98" border="0" /></a>

You need to connect each service in Inoreader to use them: Gear icon –&gt; Preferences –&gt; Integration

<a href="http://blogs.gundersons.us/josh/wp-content/uploads/sites/2/2016/05/integration-preferences-in-Inoreader.jpg"><img class="aligncenter" style="background-image: none; float: none; padding-top: 0px; padding-left: 0px; margin-left: auto; display: block; padding-right: 0px; margin-right: auto; border: 0px;" title="integration preferences in Inoreader" src="http://blogs.gundersons.us/josh/wp-content/uploads/sites/2/2016/05/integration-preferences-in-Inoreader_thumb.jpg" alt="integration preferences in Inoreader" width="244" height="234" border="0" /></a>

I will add several types of articles to each service and report the findings. I’m using Firefox 46.0.1 on Windows 10 Pro x64 RTM with latest updates.

<h4>Verdict for <em>What If?</em>:</h4>
<strong>Read it later</strong>: Instapaper or Readability – it's basically up to the user's preference between the two since the results are very similar.
<del>I need to check out and compare their Android apps to see if I lean one way or the other.</del>
[UPDATE] I prefer the Instapaper Android app over the Readability Android app and here's why:
Readability's app seems like an iOS port, only lets you choose between white or black, and none of the 5 font choices affected the yucky sans-serif font in which the text was actually displayed.
Instapaper has 4 colors (white, sepia, grey, black) and the font selection actually works.

<strong>Editable copy</strong>: OneNote.
